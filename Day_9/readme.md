Overview

Day 9 focused on building \*\*interactive Excel analysis\*\* using PivotCharts combined with slicers and timelines.  

The goal was not only to visualize data, but to apply \*\*analyst judgement\*\* on which filters add business value and which do not.



---



Key Concepts Practiced

\- Creating PivotCharts from PivotTables

\- Using slicers for categorical filtering (Gender)

\- Using Timeline for time-based filtering (Month)

\- Connecting slicers to PivotTables and PivotCharts

\- Evaluating unnecessary or misleading filters

\- Designing clean, manager-friendly layouts



---



What Was Built

\- \*\*PivotTable\*\*: Total Sales by Product Category

\- \*\*PivotChart\*\*: Column chart linked to PivotTable

\- \*\*Slicer\*\*: Gender (Male / Female)

\- \*\*Timeline\*\*: Monthly sales filtering

\- Fully interactive analysis with controlled filters



---



Key Learning Outcome

Not every field should be used as a slicer.  

Effective dashboards prioritize \*\*clarity, relevance, and business usability\*\* over excessive controls.

Skills Gained

\- Dashboard filtering logic

\- Data storytelling with visuals

\- Analyst-level decision making in Excel

\- Preparation for Power BI / Tableau dashboards
