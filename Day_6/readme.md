**Overview**

This repository contains \*\*Day 6\*\* of my Excel for Data Analytics learning journey.

The focus of this day was using \*\*Pivot Tables\*\* to transform raw retail sales data into meaningful business insights.





**Dataset**



Retail sales dataset containing:

\- Product Category

\- Gender

\- Age

\- Date

\- Quantity

\- Total Amount



**Business Questions Answered**



1\. Which product category generates the highest revenue?

2\. Which category sells the highest number of units?

3\. Which gender contributes more to total sales revenue?

4\. Which age group generates the highest total sales revenue?

5\. Which month records the highest total sales revenue?



**Key Insights**



\- \*\*Electronics\*\* generates the highest total revenue.

\- \*\*Clothing\*\* sells the highest number of units, but generates less revenue than Electronics.

\- \*\*Female customers\*\* contribute slightly higher total revenue than male customers, though contributions are balanced.

\- \*\*Older age groups\*\* generate the highest total sales revenue.

\- \*\*May\*\* is the highest revenue-generating month.



**Skills Practiced**

\- Pivot Tables (Rows, Values, Filters)

\- Revenue vs volume analysis

\- Customer segmentation analysis

\- Time-based sales analysis

\- Business insight communication



**Outcome**

This day strengthened my ability to:

\- Think in terms of business questions

\- Use Pivot Tables for fast, accurate summaries

\- Convert numbers into clear, decision-ready insights
